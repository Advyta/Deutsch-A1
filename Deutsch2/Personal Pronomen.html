Pronomen

Personal Pronomen

In German, personal pronouns are words that are used to replace specific nouns and refer to people, animals, or things. Personal pronouns simplify language by avoiding repetition and making sentences more concise. They also indicate the gender, number, and case of the noun they replace.

Nominativ Pronomen

Subject pronouns are used as the subjects of sentences, indicating who or what performs the action of the verb.

  • ich (I)
  • du (you, informal singular)
  • er (he)
  • sie (she)
  • es (it)
  • wir (we)
  • ihr (you, informal plural)
  • sie (they)
  • Sie (you, formal singular and plural)

Akkusativ pronomen

German Akkusativ pronomen (Accusative pronouns) are a type of personal pronouns used to replace and refer to direct objects in a sentence. They indicate the receiver of the action and answer the question "whom" or "what" in relation to the verb. The choice of accusative pronouns depends on the gender and number of the noun they replace.

Nominativ Akkusativ
ich mich (me)
du dich (you, informal singular)
er ihn (him)
sie sie (her)
es es (it)
wir uns (us)
ihr euch (you, informal plural)
sie sie (them)
Sie Sie (you, formal singular and plural)
