Die Artikels

Nouns refer to a person, animal, thing or concept. All nouns in German are either masculine, feminine or neuter. The little word in front of the noun, the article, will tell you the gender.

Types of Artikels

Bestimmte Artikel:

Definite articles are used when referring to specific nouns, indicating that the speaker is referring to a particular person, place, or thing.

List of Articles:

  1. Essen und Trinken
  2. Das Verkehrsmittle
  3. Der Beruf
  4. Die Familie
  5. Die Tageszeit
  6. Die Verpackungen

Unbestimmte Artikel:

Indefinite articles are used when referring to non-specific or unidentified nouns. They indicate that the speaker is talking about any one of a group or an unspecified person, place, or thing.

Possessivartikel:

German Possessivartikel (Possessive Articles) are used to indicate possession or ownership of nouns. They agree with the gender, number, and case of the noun they modify. There are two sets of Possessivartikel: strong (used without a following noun) and weak (used before a noun).

Akkusativ:

In German, the accusative case is one of the four grammatical cases used to show the role of a noun or pronoun in a sentence. The accusative case is used primarily for direct objects, that is, the nouns or pronouns that receive the action of the verb directly. When a noun is in the accusative case, it often answers the question "whom" or "what" in relation to the action of the verb.

Dativ:

In German, the dative case is one of the four grammatical cases used to indicate the role of a noun or pronoun in a sentence. The dative case is primarily used to show the indirect object, which is the recipient or beneficiary of an action. When a noun is in the dative case, it often answers the question "to whom" or "for whom" in relation to the action of the verb.
